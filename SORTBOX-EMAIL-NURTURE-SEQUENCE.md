## Email 3 — Day 7: The Story Behind SortBox

**Subject:** I used to be the messiest person I knew

Hi [Name],

I want to tell you something that's true, even though it's not the polished brand story.

I was the messiest person I knew.

Not "a bit untidy." My flat looked like I'd been evacuated from it in a hurry. Drawers I'd given up on. A kitchen I'd close the door on rather than look at. I'd been telling myself for three years that I'd "get round to sorting it."

The thing that finally changed it wasn't motivation. It wasn't a weekend project. It was a system.

Not a big system. A small, boring, specific system. One room per week. One drawer at a time. The rule was: if I could see what was in the drawer, it counted as done. When I could see all the drawers, the kitchen was done. That was the whole logic.

I used glass containers because I'd been using plastic for years and they'd all gone yellow and weird. I used drawer dividers because without them, drawers become black holes within a week. I used labels because I'd forget what I'd put where.

Six weeks later, my flat looked the way I wanted it to look. Six months later, it still did.

That's what SortBox is. It's the system I used, turned into a monthly kit, with an app to make it impossible to lose track.

If you've been putting it off — you don't need more motivation. You need a system that does the work for you.

Freddie
